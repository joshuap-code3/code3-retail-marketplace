# Code3 Content Guidelines

How to write the words that go in the template slots — titles, bullets, callouts, captions, source citations. Drawn from real Code3 QBR and capabilities work.

## Slide titles

**Titles are assertions, not descriptions.** A good title states something; a bad title names a topic.

- ❌ "Q1 Performance Overview" → ✅ "Q1 delivered +27% ROAS YoY"
- ❌ "Creator Strategy" → ✅ "Creator-led content drove 70% of Q1 spend"
- ❌ "Looking Ahead" → ✅ "Three bets for Q2"
- ❌ "Our Capabilities" → ✅ "Everything you need, under one roof"

Exception: section dividers are fine with short thematic titles ("What We Heard", "Looking Ahead", "Q1 Learnings").

**Title length:**
- Display-size titles (on slides 1–4, 39, 43, 44): 2–5 words per line, 2 lines max.
- Slide titles on content layouts: one line, usually 4–10 words.

**Case:**
- Display titles (big, on covers and dividers) — often ALL CAPS per the template. Leave the template's casing alone.
- Slide titles on content layouts — sentence case. "Q1 delivered strong ROAS growth." Not Title Case. Not ALL CAPS.
- Eyebrow labels (small text above a title) — ALL CAPS. Always short: "Q1 PERFORMANCE", "STRATEGY RECAP".

## Body copy

Keep body short. A slide is a conversation aid, not a document.

- **Paragraphs:** 2–4 sentences max. Most slides have room for 50–120 words of body. Watch the placeholder length guidance in `SLIDE_INDEX.md`.
- **Bullets:** 1 line each when possible, 2 lines max. No sub-bullets in template slides.
- **Sentence length:** Prefer short, direct sentences. Mix in a longer one for rhythm, but default to ~15 words.

## Bullets and key takeaways

Code3 uses "big sentence" bullet layouts (slides 13, 14) a lot. Each bullet is a declarative sentence, not a fragment.

- ❌ "Creator content", "eRetail push", "New ASINs"
- ✅ "Creator-led content grew engagement 27% YoY."
- ✅ "eRetail spend tripled, driven by Amazon DSP."
- ✅ "Three new ASINs launched into Amazon subscribe-and-save."

**Parallel structure** in every bullet list. Same subject type, same tense, same length neighborhood.

## Numbers and stats

- **Always contextualize.** "+27% YoY" not "27%". "vs. benchmark" or "vs. prior quarter" where applicable.
- **Hierarchy on stat slides.** One big headline stat → supporting metrics (medium) → benchmark comparison (small).
- **Directional cues.** ↑ ↓ or explicit +/– prefixes help scannability.
- **Round appropriately.** 27.4% on a slide is fine; 27.38% is noise. Keep one decimal max for rates, whole numbers for spend where possible.
- **Currency.** Spell it the first time ("$2.3M in Q1 media spend"), then shorthand ("$2.3M") after. Avoid inconsistent comma/period formatting — pick one and stick to it across the deck.
- **Units matter.** ROAS, CPM, CTR, NTB%, DPVR, VVR, LAL — these are Code3-native metrics. Use them naturally but don't spell out acronyms people in the room don't know.

## Source citations

**Mandatory on any slide with data.** This is Code3 house style. Every chart, stat grid, table gets a source.

Format options seen in real decks:
- `Source: Helix | Q1 2026`
- `Data represented 1/1 – 2/28`
- `Source: [Platform] | [Date range]`

Place the source in the slide's designated footer/caption area. If the layout doesn't have one, put it as a short line below the data, smaller type.

When numbers come from multiple sources in one slide, cite them all. Don't hide methodology.

**Exceptions (so the "always cite" rule doesn't fight the layouts):**
- **Layouts with no caption/footer slot** (the big-sentence pill takeaways, section dividers, executive-summary pills) inherit their source from the section's data slide. Don't try to jam a citation where there's no slot — cite on the data slide that the takeaway summarizes.
- **Refresh mode:** preserve the source deck's existing citation state. If the original deck has no citations, don't add citation text boxes (that violates the no-new-shapes rule) — flag the gap in the delivery note instead.

## Callouts and sidebars

Several template slides have callout boxes (usually pills or rounded rectangles in green, blue, or black). Use callouts for:

- A single supporting stat that amplifies the main point ("+35% CTR on the responsive creative")
- A verbatim quote from the client or a user
- A short "so what" sentence when the main body leaves the implication implicit

Callout copy is tight: 5–20 words. Not a paragraph.

## Section eyebrows

Many content slides have a small ALL-CAPS label above the title (e.g. "SECTION TITLE"). Replace this with the current section name so the reader always knows where they are in the deck.

- ❌ Leave as "SECTION TITLE"
- ✅ "Q1 LEARNINGS" or "LOOKING AHEAD"

Keep eyebrows to 1–3 words.

## Captions and image treatments

If the template has an image caption slot, use it to anchor the image — why is this picture here?

- ❌ Leave empty or use "Image caption"
- ✅ "Creator @mayasellscosmetics drove the highest CTR in Q1."

Don't caption logos (the brand is its own caption).

## Dates and timeframes

- **Months:** spell out or abbreviate consistently. "April 2026" on title slides, "Apr 2026" is fine in compact layouts.
- **Quarters:** "Q1 2026", not "1Q26" (client-readable).
- **Date ranges:** "1/1 – 2/28" or "Jan 1 – Feb 28" — match the deck's other date formatting.

## Client naming

- Use the client's actual brand name consistently, exactly as they write it. If it's "Amlactin" (not "AmLactin"), spell it that way every time.
- For multi-brand accounts, use the same order throughout (e.g. Amlactin | Kerasal | Triple Paste).
- Don't name competitor clients in a client-facing deck.
- Code3's own name: "Code3", not "CODE3" in running copy (all-caps is only for the wordmark/logo and display typography).

## Things Code3 never does in client decks

- Doesn't use "Dear [Client]" style openings on slides — this isn't a letter.
- Doesn't thank the reader on a slide ("Thank you for your time") — replace with a concrete closing like "Let's talk about Q2" or "What's next".
- Doesn't use generic business clichés: "unlock potential," "at the end of the day," "at the intersection of" (except when actually talking about the Code3 DNA which literally uses "intersection"), "delve," "navigate the landscape," "holistic approach."
- Doesn't end on a whimper. Last slide should state the next action or the core recommendation, not just say thanks.

## When content doesn't fit

Priority order:
1. Cut words — tighter is almost always better.
2. Split into two slides — one idea per slide is fine.
3. Pick a roomier layout — e.g. single paragraph instead of 5-bullet list.
4. Pick a tighter layout — if the content is thin, don't inflate it to fill a spacious layout; switch to a minimal one (slide 20 or 18).
5. Only as a last resort: combine ideas.

Never: resize text on a template slide to cram more in. That's the v1 failure mode.
