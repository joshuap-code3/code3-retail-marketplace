# Code3 Deck Recipes

Use these as starting-point skeletons — not rigid templates. Each recipe lists which slides (by number in `assets/code3_template_core.pptx`, per `assets/SLIDE_INDEX.md`) to include, in what order, and the kind of content that belongs on each. Always read `brand.md` before writing copy, and adapt the recipe to the actual content you have.

The recipes below are distilled from real Code3 QBR and capabilities work. They are **starting points**, not constraints. If a section doesn't apply, drop it; if the user's content needs something extra, add it.

---

## Pattern principles (apply to every deck)

1. **Every slide earns its place.** Code3's QBR analysis showed there are no context-only slides. If a slide doesn't advance the story or deliver an insight, kill it.

2. **Problem → Response → Results.** This three-beat structure shows up across Code3 decks. Lead each section with the challenge, then what was done about it, then what happened. Use it at the deck level and at the section level.

3. **Data always has a source and a date range.** "Source: Helix | Q1 2026" or "Data represented 1/1–2/28" appears throughout real Code3 work. Never show a number without context.

4. **Parallel structure when grouping things.** Three brands side by side get the same slide template three times, same metric framing, same vertical order. This makes comparisons legible and signals discipline.

5. **Section dividers are mandatory for decks >8 slides.** They create breathing room and signal transitions. Slide 7 (dark, with image) or Slide 8 (text-only) both work. Slide 9 (section break with tagline) is good for pivot moments ("Where we go for answers").

6. **Vary layout across a section.** Even within a 5-slide section, don't use the same content layout 5 times. Mix one-column paragraphs, two-column content, and a stat/chart slide if you have data.

7. **Open and close hard.** Title cover should be sharp — the title itself conveys the deck's point, not a generic "Q2 Review." The closing slide should state what's next and invite action; don't just say "Thank you."

8. **Match content volume to the box.** The boxed three-column layouts (slides 22–24) center body text vertically, so a one-sentence blurb in a tall box looks half-empty and ragged next to fuller columns. If column content is short, give each column comparable length (~2–4 sentences), or switch to a denser/tighter layout. Don't leave a tall box mostly white. Also note: a Kalam source/callout brace on these layouts often sits under the *middle* column only — if it's meant to cover all three, that reads as applying to just one; keep such a brace's scope unambiguous.

9. **Closing slides: skip empty device mockups.** The closing layout (slide 45) and the mobile-mockup layouts (20, 27–28) carry fixed phone graphics whose screens render **blank** unless you supply real screenshots — a closer with blank phones reads unfinished. For a CTA/closing without real screenshots, use a text-forward close (slide 45's text side, or a key-takeaways/divider layout). And keep the closing pill/eyebrow label short: a long one like "What we recommend for Q2" overflows the pill.

## Recipe: QBR (Quarterly Business Review)

The Code3 QBR arc (from the Advantice QBR analysis):

1. **Cover** (slide 1–4) — client logo, deck title (usually "[Client] QBR | [Quarter]"), month/year
2. **Agenda** (slide 5 or 6) — 5 numbered items. Typical shape: Period Snapshot · Strategy Recap · Learnings · Looking Ahead · Appendix
3. **Section divider: "Q[N] Performance Snapshot"** (slide 7 or 8)
4. **Performance callout slide** (slide 30 or 31) — brand-by-brand grid: ROAS, spend, CTR, NTB%. Color-code by brand. Benchmarks and directional arrows mandatory.
5. **Performance deep-dive slide per brand** (slide 22 or 23, 3-column) — for multi-brand accounts. Left column: brand name + positioning. Center: what we ran. Right: outcome.
6. **Section divider: "Q[N] Strategy Recap"**
7. **Strategy-by-brand slide** (slide 22 or 23) — "Social-forward, creator-led, eRetail emphasis" style three-column framing.
8. **Channel or tactic deep-dive slides** (slide 19 or 20 for image-led, slide 17 for bulleted findings)
9. **Creative deep-dive** (slide 20 with image; use phone-mockup slides 27–29 only if the client does paid social)
10. **Section divider: "Q[N] Learnings"** — introduce with "↑ Tailwinds / ↓ Headwinds" framing
11. **Tailwinds slide** (slide 11 or 13) — 3–4 big sentences, each a single learning
12. **Headwinds slide** (slide 11 or 13) — parallel structure

> Note on the big-sentence pill layouts: they come in 4-pill (pos 13, blue) and 5-pill (pos 14, coral) only. If you have exactly 3 tailwinds or headwinds, do NOT leave blank pills — use a 3-column problem→response layout (pos 22) instead, or split across slides. Tailwind and headwind counts often differ between sections; that's expected, just don't force a count into the wrong-sized grid.
13. **Test results slide** (slide 32 or 33) — if A/B or creative tests ran
14. **Section divider: "Looking Ahead"** or "Q[N+1] Playbook"
15. **Strategic priorities slide** (slide 23 or 24 for 3-column, or slide 25/26 for pairs) — 3–5 priorities
16. **Big bets slide** (slide 11 or 13) — 3–5 big statements
17. **Channel plan / NPD launches** (slide 34 for timeline table, slide 35 for timeline-with-rows, or slide 22 for parallel)
18. **Closing "what's next" slide** (slide 45) — current state vs. recommendation
19. **Appendix divider** (slide 7 or 8) — if you have backup data
20. **Appendix slides** — whatever tactical detail needed

Target length: 20–35 slides for a quarterly review.

**Key QBR voice moves:**
- Titles are assertions, not descriptions: "Creator content drove 70% of YTD spend" not "Creator content overview"
- "↑ Tailwinds / ↓ Headwinds" to frame learnings positively
- Every section ends pointing forward
- Brand-specific labeling on every insight when it's a multi-brand account

## Recipe: New-Business Pitch

Used to pitch a prospective client. Lean on Code3's company story and capabilities slides (39–44).

1. **Cover** (slide 3 or 4) — title should be a promise, e.g. "Putting rocket fuel under [Client] in 2026" not "New Business Pitch"
2. **Greeting / Why we're here** (slide 9 — section break with tagline) — "We know [Client's business]. Here's what we'd do first."
3. **Section: Who We Are** — brief, don't belabor
4. **"Hey, we're Code3"** (slide 39) — company narrative
5. **DNA / values** (slide 40) — "It's in our DNA": obsession, partnering, focus
6. **Integrated offer** (slide 41) — 4 quadrants: Creative, Connections, Data, Commerce
7. **Capabilities grid** (slide 44) — 12-service matrix
8. **Company We Keep** — if we have it, a logos slide; otherwise skip
9. **Section: What We Heard / What We'd Do** (slide 8 section divider)
10. **What we heard** (slide 17 — body with callout, or slide 11 for 3 key points) — mirror the client's stated problem back to them
11. **What we'd do** (slide 22 or 23) — 3 columns of recommended approach
12. **Section: Proof / Case study** (slide 8)
13. **One or two case studies** — pick the template's existing case-study layouts, edit heavily (or use slide 20/19 with new image + text). Structure: Client problem → Approach → Results.
14. **Section: How we'd partner**
15. **Team slide** (slide 15 or 20 with image) — named Code3 team for this account
16. **Timeline / first 90 days** (slide 35 or 34) — what happens when
17. **Section: Investment**
18. **Pricing slide** (slide 20 or 17 body layout; pricing tables typically live in an appendix or follow-up document rather than the pitch deck itself)
19. **Closing** (slide 45) — call to action: "Let's get to work" or similar

Target length: 25–35 slides.

**Pitch voice moves:**
- Tailor opening to what you heard from the client. Don't lead with company intro.
- "We'd" not "we will" — it's hypothetical until signed.
- Use the DNA/Connections-Creative-Commerce framing — it's the Code3 integrated-agency pitch.

## Recipe: Capabilities Overview

A stand-alone deck describing what Code3 does. Less tailored than a pitch; often shared as a leave-behind.

1. **Cover** (slide 1 or 2) — "Code3 Capabilities [Year]"
2. **"Hey, we're Code3"** (slide 39) — narrative intro
3. **DNA / values** (slide 40)
4. **Integrated offer** (slide 41)
5. **Capabilities grid** (slide 44)
6. **Case study 1** — slide 19 or 20 (image + narrative)
7. **Case study 2** — same structure
8. **Case study 3** — same structure
9. **Timeline / company story** (slide 43) — 2009–present milestones
10. **Closing** (slide 45) — how to start a conversation

Target length: 12–20 slides. Keep it tight.

## Recipe: Strategy Recommendation

A deck that makes a specific recommendation — channel shift, budget reallocation, creative direction, etc.

1. **Cover** (slide 1 or 2) — title is the recommendation in a sentence, e.g. "Shift 30% of paid spend to creator content in Q3"
2. **Recap of the brief / Brief recap of the brief** (slide 11 body slide) — reset context in 1 paragraph
3. **Executive summary** (slide 12 — 3 key-point bullets) — "We recommend X because Y. Here's how, and here's the expected impact."
4. **Section divider: "What We Found"**
5. **3–5 finding slides** — each one is a single insight with supporting data. Mix slide 17 (bullets + callout), slide 20 (image + body), slide 30/31 (stat grids).
6. **Section divider: "What We Recommend"**
7. **The recommendation, broken out** — slide 22 or 23 (3-column: what to do, why it works, how to measure)
8. **Expected impact** (slide 32 or 33) — stat or chart
9. **Section divider: "How We'd Do It"**
10. **Rollout / timeline** (slide 34 or 35)
11. **Section divider: "What We Need From You"** (only if ask involves client effort)
12. **Asks slide** (slide 11 or 13) — 3–5 concrete asks
13. **Closing** (slide 45)

Target length: 15–25 slides.

**Recommendation voice moves:**
- Title slide IS the recommendation. No one should have to guess the point.
- Show your work — findings support recommendation. Don't leap.
- Quantify expected impact. Every recommendation should project what good looks like.

## Recipe: Kickoff

First meeting after a contract signs. Set expectations, confirm scope, introduce the team.

1. **Cover** (slide 1) — "Let's get started. [Client] × Code3"
2. **Agenda** (slide 5)
3. **Section: Quick reintro**
4. **Who's in the room** (slide 39 for the Code3 story, or skip if already established)
5. **Your team** (team slide — use slide 20 or 15 with image + bios)
6. **Section: What we heard**
7. **Recap of scope / brief** (slide 11 body)
8. **What success looks like** (slide 12 — 3-bullet summary)
9. **Section: Plan for Q1 / first 90 days**
10. **Workstreams** (slide 22 or 23 — 3 column)
11. **Timeline / milestones** (slide 34 or 35)
12. **Meeting cadence** (slide 25 or 26 — 2-column pairs)
13. **Section: How we'll measure**
14. **KPIs** (slide 30 or 31 — stat grid)
15. **Reporting** (slide 11 body)
16. **Section: Asks**
17. **Asks** (slide 13)
18. **Closing** (slide 45)

Target length: 15–20 slides.

## Recipe: One-off / General-purpose client deck

When the user has a brief that doesn't fit the above — a one-off strategic POV, a workshop readout, a research synthesis, a response to a specific client ask.

Pattern to default to (adjust freely):

1. **Cover** (slide 1–4)
2. **Agenda** (slide 5 or 6) if >8 content slides; skip if shorter
3. **Context / brief recap** (slide 11)
4. **Main section 1** — divider (slide 7/8) + 3–6 content slides
5. **Main section 2** — divider + 3–6 content slides
6. **Main section 3** — divider + 3–6 content slides (if needed)
7. **Key takeaways** (slide 13 or 14) — 3–5 big sentences
8. **Next steps** (slide 11 body or slide 13)
9. **Closing** (slide 45) — or skip if a single-section deck

## Slide-picking decision tree

When the content needs a home, use this to pick a layout:

| The deck's title + client + date | Slide 1–4 (pick based on image availability) |
| A list of 5 agenda items | Slide 5 (with icons) or 6 (text-only) |
| A section title (between major sections) | Slide 7 (with image), 8 (text-only), or 9 (with tagline) |
| A transition question | Slide 10 ("So… what now?" style) |
| A brief recap paragraph | Slide 11 |
| 3 key takeaway sentences | Slide 12 |
| 4–5 key takeaway sentences (bigger) | Slide 13 (4) or 14 (5) |
| Image + longer narrative | Slide 15 or 16 |
| Body with a title header + 3–5 bullets + callout | Slide 17 |
| A single paragraph under a title | Slide 18 or 20 |
| 2 side-by-side things with body | Slide 19 |
| A phone-app screenshot + narrative | Slide 20 or 27–29 (ONLY if deck is about social/mobile) |
| 3 parallel items, each with subtitle + body | Slide 22 or 24 |
| 3 parallel items with subtitle + subtext + body | Slide 23 |
| 4 paired items (label + body) in 2 cols | Slide 26 |
| 3 paired items (label + body) in 2 cols | Slide 25 |
